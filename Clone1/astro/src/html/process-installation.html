Regulatory Compliance & Risk

Stay ahead of evolving ESG regulations across jurisdictions with proactive gap assessments, readiness programs, and ongoing monitoring systems — so you're not just reacting to regulation but prepared for what comes next.

The pace of ESG-related regulation has accelerated dramatically. What was once voluntary guidelines is hardening into mandatory disclosure obligations, due diligence duties, and enforceable penalties across multiple jurisdictions.

The Regulatory Landscape

European Union: CSRD (detailed ESRS disclosures, limited assurance, XBRL tagging), EU Taxonomy (green activity classification), CSDDD (human rights and environmental due diligence with director liability), and EUDR (deforestation-free supply chains).

United States: SEC Climate Rule (climate risk disclosure, Scope 1 & 2 emissions) and California SB 253/261 (Scope 1-3 disclosure, TCFD-aligned reporting).

Other Jurisdictions: UK SDR, Modern Slavery Acts, Germany's LkSG, France's Duty of Vigilance, and supply chain due diligence laws across Europe.

Our Approach

Horizon Scanning: Map your regulatory exposure across every jurisdiction where you operate, sell, or source — with a regulatory calendar turning uncertainty into planning.

Gap Assessment: Evaluate your current state against each applicable regulation. We prioritize gaps by penalty risk, reputational exposure, and implementation effort.

Remediation Roadmap: Specific actions, owners, deadlines, and budget estimates for every gap — from drafting policies to training teams and engaging third-party providers.

Ongoing Monitoring: Quarterly briefings on developments relevant to your profile, updated gap assessments as rules change, and early warnings about upcoming deadlines.

Deliverables:
